About

The color orange is a vibrant and energetic hue that lies between red and yellow in the visible spectrum. It is often associated with warmth, enthusiasm, and creativity. The color's psychological impact suggests feelings of optimism and vitality. In nature, orange is prevalent in sunsets, fruits like oranges and pumpkins, conveying a sense of energy and change. Culturally, it is used to symbolize joy, balance, and determination. Whether in art, design, or everyday objects, the presence of orange tends to evoke a sense of vibrancy and playfulness, making it a versatile and dynamic color.

Types

Seville Orange: Seville oranges are known for their bitter taste, making them unsuitable for direct consumption. The bitter notes contribute a unique and complex profile to various dishes.

Bergamot Orange: Bergamot oranges are renowned for their fragrant peel, which is a key component in producing bergamot essential oil. Beyond tea, bergamot oranges find applications in culinary creations, lending their unique flavor to certain dishes.

Trifoliate Orange: Crifoliate oranges are notable for their cold-hardy nature, making them suitable for regions with colder climates. While the fruit is too sour for direct consumption, trifoliate oranges are valued for their ornamental appeal.
